# Code of Conduct

> **Anime Calendar v2 is an open-source Anime Release Intelligence Platform dedicated to accuracy, transparency, personalization, and long-term maintainability.**

## Our commitment

We are committed to a welcoming, respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free community for everyone, regardless of experience, identity, background, ability, or role.

## Expected behavior

Participants should:

- communicate with respect and patience;
- critique ideas and code rather than people;
- assume good intent while remaining open to correction;
- welcome questions from new contributors;
- credit sources and other contributors;
- disclose uncertainty and conflicts of interest;
- prioritize user safety, privacy, and trust;
- accept moderation decisions made to protect the community.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following are not tolerated:

- harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or personal attacks;
- sexualized language or unwanted attention;
- doxxing or sharing private information;
- threats or encouragement of harm;
- sustained disruption, trolling, or bad-faith participation;
- plagiarism or deliberate source misrepresentation;
- attempts to introduce malware, credentials, piracy infrastructure, or unsafe code;
- retaliation against someone who reports a concern.

## Scope

This code applies in project repositories, discussions, reviews, issue trackers, community spaces, and public interactions made on behalf of the project.

## Reporting and enforcement

Concerns should be reported privately to the repository owner or designated maintainers through a private GitHub security advisory or another private channel listed by the project. Do not include sensitive personal information in a public issue.

Maintainers may remove content, close discussions, reject contributions, issue warnings, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban participants based on severity and pattern of behavior.

Reports will be handled with discretion. Retaliation is prohibited.

## Good-faith disagreement

Technical disagreement is healthy. Contributors are expected to explain evidence, tradeoffs, and uncertainty. Firm critique is acceptable; contempt and personal hostility are not.

## Attribution

This policy is inspired by established open-source community conduct standards and is intentionally written in project-specific language.
